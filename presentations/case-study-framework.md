# Case Study Reframing Framework

## The Problem with Traditional Case Studies

Traditional case studies are framed around **upside**:

> "We helped Client X grow their revenue by 42% in 6 months"

This forces the buyer to imagine their current situation changing to a better future based on a **possibility**. But they aren't currently experiencing that pain — so it's just a vague claim to consider.

**Which makes it optional.**

They can appreciate it without needing to pay you because nothing about their current reality is forcing them to move forward.

---

## Why This Matters in B2B (and GovCon)

In B2B environments:
- Careers are always on the line
- Political decisions are being made
- **No one gets fired for missing a "growth opportunity"**
- **People definitely get fired for missing something that could have been prevented**

If your case studies are framed around growth, they're still optional.

---

## The Reframe: From Growth to Hidden Loss

Instead of proving you can produce upside, prove you **see what others are missing**.

The fastest way to prove you know what you're doing in B2B is to **expose a hidden loss that feels uncomfortable when it's revealed**.

The buyer immediately identifies it because it's the lie they've been avoiding for years. And it becomes the purest driver of urgency.

---

## Formula Comparison

### Traditional (Optional)
| Stage | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Before | "Client was at X" |
| After | "Now they're at Y" |
| Result | "Z% improvement" |

### Reframed (Urgent)
| Stage | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Hidden Problem** | What they didn't know was broken |
| **Escalation** | How it was silently getting worse |
| **Near Failure** | The cost of continuing (career risk, money lost) |
| **Intervention** | What we did to stop the bleed |
| **Stabilization** | The loss that was prevented |

---

## Example: Marketing Agency

### Traditional Version (Weak)
> "We helped Client X optimize their funnel and increase conversions by 30%, generating $3.5M in additional pipeline over 5 months."

### Reframed Version (Urgent)
> "Client was spending $85K/month on paid channels and thought performance was 'fine.'
>
> But their funnel was underconverting by 30% relative to benchmark, which meant 140 qualified buyers per month were being lost silently.
>
> Over a 5-month period, that translated to roughly $3.5M in missed pipeline.
>
> The issue wasn't traffic — it was how demand was being processed post-click.
>
> Once we rebuilt the conversion layer, we stopped the $3.5M bleed within 90 days."

## GovCon Case Study Examples

### Example 1: Missed Recompete (BD Training)

**Traditional:**
> "We trained their BD team and they won a $4M recompete."

**Reframed:**
> "Client had a $4M contract expiring in 14 months. Their ops team was executing flawlessly — but no one was tracking the recompete timeline.
>
> By the time BD got involved, the incumbent had already been meeting with the CO for 6 months. They were 8 months behind on relationship-building.
>
> We ran their ops leads through BD fundamentals. Within 60 days, they identified 3 follow-on signals the BD team had missed entirely.
>
> They held the contract. But they were 30 days from losing $4M because ops and BD weren't talking."

---

### Example 2: Set-Aside Blindness (Market Intelligence)

**Traditional:**
> "We helped them find a $2.5M opportunity they wouldn't have seen otherwise."

**Reframed:**
> "Client was bidding full-and-open competitions against large primes and losing on price every time.
>
> They had SDVOSB certification sitting unused. There were 23 SDVOSB set-asides in their NAICS posted in the last 90 days — they'd responded to zero.
>
> That's $47M in addressable pipeline they didn't know existed.
>
> We rebuilt their opportunity monitoring. First qualified set-aside they pursued: $2.5M win against 4 competitors instead of 40."

---

### Example 3: Proposal Churn (Capture Process)

**Traditional:**
> "We improved their win rate from 15% to 35%."

**Reframed:**
> "Client was submitting 20 proposals a year with a 15% win rate. That sounds fine until you calculate the cost.
>
> Each proposal cost them roughly $25K in labor. 17 losses per year = $425K spent on proposals that went nowhere.
>
> The problem wasn't the proposals. It was qualification — they were bidding on everything instead of the right things.
>
> We installed a capture process with gate reviews. Submissions dropped to 12/year. Wins went from 3 to 5.
>
> Same win count, but they recovered $200K in wasted proposal costs."

---

### Example 4: CMMC Wake-Up Call (Compliance)

**Traditional:**
> "We helped them achieve CMMC Level 2 compliance."

**Reframed:**
> "Client had a $6M DoD contract with 18 months remaining. They assumed CMMC wouldn't affect them until renewal.
>
> Then the CO sent a letter: CMMC Level 2 required for the next task order. 90-day deadline.
>
> They weren't close. No SSP, no POAM, gaps in 14 of 110 controls. At risk of losing $6M and their past performance reference.
>
> We ran a 60-day sprint. They passed assessment with 5 days to spare.
>
> They kept the contract — but they were 60 days from a career-ending loss."

---

### Example 5: Teaming Failure (Subcontracting)

**Traditional:**
> "We helped them secure a subcontracting role on a $50M IDIQ."

**Reframed:**
> "Client had been sending capability statements to primes for 3 years. Zero callbacks.
>
> The problem: they were leading with what they wanted to sell, not what the prime needed.
>
> One prime they'd approached 4 times had a CMMC compliance gap on an active contract. Client had the exact capability. But their cap statement never mentioned it.
>
> We rebuilt their approach: research the prime's gaps, show up with a solution.
>
> First targeted outreach: $8M subcontract on a $50M vehicle. Same capability they'd been pitching for 3 years."

## How to Apply This

For every case study you have:

1. **Find the hidden problem** — What didn't they know was broken?
2. **Calculate the escalation** — How much was it costing them silently?
3. **Identify the near failure** — What career risk or financial loss was coming?
4. **Describe your intervention** — What specifically did you do?
5. **Quantify the stabilization** — What loss was prevented?

The proof becomes that you **see what others miss** — not that you can produce upside.
