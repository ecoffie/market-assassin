How to Set Up SAM.gov Alerts

1

Login to SAM.gov

Go to sam.gov and click "Sign In" in the top right. Use your Login.gov credentials (or create a free account).

2

Navigate to Contract Opportunities

Click "Contract Opportunities" from the main menu, then click "Search" to access the opportunity search page.

3

Configure Your Filters

Set up your search criteria. For A/E/C contractors, use these filters:

  • NAICS: 236220, 237110, 237310, 237990, 541330
  • Set-Aside: Small Business, 8(a), SDVOSB, HUBZone, WOSB
  • State: FL, GA, AL, SC (your target states)
  • Agency: DEPT OF DEFENSE, DEPT OF VETERANS AFFAIRS
4

Save Your Search

Click "Save Search" at the top of the results. Name it something memorable like "AEC Florida Opportunities". Select "Daily" for email frequency.

5

Create Multiple Saved Searches

Don't rely on one search. Create separate alerts for different opportunity types:

  • "USACE Construction SAT" - Under $350K, USACE only
  • "NAVFAC MACCs" - Keywords: MACC, MATOC, IDIQ
  • "VA Healthcare Construction" - VA + NAICS 236220
  • "Sources Sought AEC" - Notice Type: Sources Sought only
  • "Competitor Watch" - Your competitors' names as keywords

Pro Tips

  • Separate Sources Sought from Solicitations - Sources Sought require different responses and timing
  • Set up competitor alerts - Use competitor names as keywords to see what they're bidding
  • Include "Design-Build" and "DB" - These won't always have NAICS 236220
  • Check daily - Email alerts can be delayed; check SAM.gov directly each morning

How to Track Recompetes on USASpending

1

Go to USASpending.gov

Navigate to usaspending.gov and click "Advanced Search" → "Contracts"

2

Set Your Filters

  • NAICS Code: 236220 (or 237310, 541330)
  • Awarding Agency: Department of Defense (or specific agency)
  • Time Period: Contracts ending in next 6-18 months
3

Export to CSV

Click "Download" to export the results. Open in Excel/Google Sheets to build your tracking spreadsheet.

4

Key Columns to Analyze

  • recipient_name = Current incumbent
  • current_total_value_of_award = Contract size
  • period_of_performance_current_end_date = When to start engaging
  • number_of_offers_received = Competition level
  • awarding_agency_name = Buyer to research

Timeline Rule of Thumb

  • 12-18 months out: Start building relationships with CO and incumbent
  • 9-12 months out: Respond to Sources Sought notices
  • 6-9 months out: RFP typically drops
  • 3-6 months out: Proposal writing and submission

SAT Sweet Spot Strategy

  • Target $50K - $350K contracts - Typically 5-10 bidders vs 40+ on larger contracts
  • Awards happen fast - Often 14-30 days from solicitation close
  • Build past performance - Stack 3-5 SAT contracts, then bid larger work
  • Relationships matter more - Fewer competitors = CO remembers you

Prompt 1: Analyze an RFP

Analyze this RFP and tell me:
1. What are the evaluation factors and their weights?
2. What past performance is required (contract size, relevance, recency)?
3. What certifications or clearances are needed?
4. What are the key compliance requirements I can't miss?
5. What questions should I ask at the industry day?
6. Who is the likely incumbent and what are their weaknesses?

Format it professionally with sections for: Core Competencies, Past Performance,

Prompt 3: Research a Competitor

Research [Competitor Name] as a federal contractor:

1. What agencies do they primarily work with?
2. What NAICS codes do they operate under?
3. What contract vehicles are they on (MACC, MATOC, IDIQ)?
4. What major contracts are expiring in the next 18 months?

A/E/C GovCon Glossary

SAT Simplified Acquisition Threshold - Currently $350,000. Contracts below this can use streamlined procedures.
MACC Multiple Award Construction Contract - IDIQ vehicle where multiple contractors compete for task orders.
MATOC Multiple Award Task Order Contract - Similar to MACC, commonly used by USACE.
IDIQ Indefinite Delivery/Indefinite Quantity - Contract type where specific quantities are ordered over time.
D-B / DB Design-Build - Project delivery method where one entity provides both design and construction.
D-B-B Design-Bid-Build - Traditional delivery: separate design, then competitive construction bid.
MILCON Military Construction - Congressionally-authorized construction projects for DoD.
Sources Sought Market research notice asking industry for capability information before issuing an RFP.
SBLO Small Business Liaison Officer - Agency contact to help small businesses understand opportunities.
OSBP Office of Small Business Programs - Agency office responsible for small business goals.
CPARS Contractor Performance Assessment Reporting System - Government's official past performance database.
FAR Federal Acquisition Regulation - Primary rules governing federal procurement.
DFARS Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement - Additional rules for DoD contracts.
8(a) SBA program for socially/economically disadvantaged small businesses. Allows sole-source awards up to $4M.
SDVOSB Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Business - Certification for veteran-owned companies.
HUBZone Historically Underutilized Business Zone - Certification based on location and employee residence.
