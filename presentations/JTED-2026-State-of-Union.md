#### 2A. FAR OVERHAUL - WHAT YOU NEED TO KNOW

**The Big Picture:**
- Executive Order 14275 (April 2025): Overhaul the entire FAR
- Phase 1: Class deviations taking effect NOW (Feb 2026)
- Phase 2: Formal rulemaking coming

**THE RULE OF TWO IS SAFE** ✅
- Despite fears, it survived and is now reinforced in FAR 19.104-1
- "MUST set aside" for small business if two or more can compete
- This was NOT a given - industry pushed hard for this

**But Here's What Changed:**

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Socioeconomic set-asides were emphasized | Now they say "MAY" not "MUST" |
| 8(a) contracts stayed 8(a) forever | 8(a) can now move to HUBZone, SDVOSB, WOSB |
| Recertification at order level | Locked in at contract level |
| Multiple award orders followed rule of two | Now discretionary (can't protest) |

**Eric's Practical Insight:**
> "What does this mean for you? If you're 8(a), you're still in the game - but you're now competing against other socioeconomic categories on follow-ons. If you're HUBZone or SDVOSB, doors just opened that were previously locked."

> "The contractors I see winning right now aren't waiting for the CO to set something aside - they're going in EARLY and making the case. Don't be passive."

**Source:** [FAR Part 19 Overhaul - Holland & Knight](https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2025/09/a-new-era-for-small-business-key-takeaways-from-the-far)

---

#### 2B. NDAA FY2026 - THE GAME CHANGERS

**Compliance Burden SLASHED:**

The big news is **Cost Accounting Standards (CAS)** - the rules that require formal accounting systems, consistent cost allocation, and expensive compliance infrastructure.

| Threshold | Before | After | What It Means |
|-----------|--------|-------|---------------|
| CAS Full Coverage | $50M | **$100M** | Only kicks in at $100M+ now |
| CAS Contract Applicability | $2.5M | **$35M** | Most SB contracts now exempt |

**Why This Matters:**

Before, if you won a $3M contract with CAS requirements, you needed:
- Formal cost accounting system
- Consistent overhead allocation methods
- Audit-ready books
- Often $30-50K+ in accounting/compliance setup

Now? That same $3M contract - no CAS. You can bid on $10M, $20M, even $30M contracts without triggering these requirements.

**Eric's Practical Insight:**
> "I've talked to contractors who passed on bigger opportunities because they didn't want the CAS headache. That excuse is gone now. If you've been staying small to avoid compliance complexity - the ceiling just got a lot higher."

> "This is the government saying: we want you to grow. Take them up on it."

**Commercial is NOW the Default:**
- DoD must now START with commercial procedures
- Senior-level documentation required to go non-commercial
- Prime contractor compliance burdens don't automatically flow down to subs

**Past Performance Revolution:**
- Commercial contract performance can now count
- Alternative evaluation methods allowed for new requirements
- Reduces incumbent advantage

**Frivolous Protest Penalty:**
- If GAO dismisses your protest as baseless: 5% payment withholding
- Designed to stop incumbents from gaming the system

**What Got Cut:**
- SBIR/STTR programs NOT reauthorized (funding lapsed Oct 2025)
- If you relied on SBIR, this is a problem

**Source:** [FY2026 NDAA Key Takeaways - PilieroMazza](https://www.pilieromazza.com/fiscal-year-2026-ndaa-signed-into-law-6-key-takeaways-for-defense-contractors/)

#### 2C. CMMC 2.0 - THE ELEPHANT IN THE ROOM

**Current Status:**
- Rule finalized, rollout underway

**Eric's Practical Insight:**

### SECTION 4: HOW TO WIN (10 minutes)

**Opening:**
> "I've given you the what and the where. Now let me give you the how - based on what I see working every single day."

---

#### 4A. THE AI ADVANTAGE

**Eric's Observations:**
> "I'm not going to tell you AI will replace you. But I will tell you that contractors using AI are outpacing those who aren't."

**Where AI is Making a Difference:**
1. **Proposal writing** - First drafts in hours, not days
2. **Capture research** - Analyzing SAM.gov, FPDS, USASpending data
3. **Past performance mining** - Finding relevant citations you forgot about
4. **Compliance checking** - CMMC gap analysis, FAR clause review

**Eric's Practical Insight:**
> "I had a contractor tell me they used to spend 80 hours on a proposal. Now it's 30. Same quality. That's 50 hours they can spend on the next opportunity."

> "But here's the thing - AI doesn't replace relationships. It frees you up to build more of them."

---

#### 4B. SOCIAL MEDIA & VISIBILITY

**What I See Working:**
> "The contractors winning right now are visible. They're not hiding behind their capability statements."

**LinkedIn Strategy:**
1. **Share your wins** - Every award, every milestone
2. **Comment on agency posts** - COs are watching
3. **Publish thought leadership** - Pain points you can solve
4. **Engage with primes** - They're always looking for subs

**Eric's Practical Insight:**
> "I've seen small businesses get on Source Sought lists because a CO saw their LinkedIn post. It sounds crazy, but visibility matters. The old 'stealth mode' approach doesn't work anymore."

---

#### 4C. THE TEAMING IMPERATIVE

**The Math:**
- Primes need small business subcontracting to meet goals
- Navy SeaPort NxG: **85% small business participation** (1,023 contracts)
- DoD goal: 23% to small business

**How to Approach Primes:**
1. **Know what they need** - Not what you want to sell
2. **Come with a capability brief** - 1 page, specific to their contract
3. **Offer to solve a problem** - CMMC compliance, surge capacity, etc.
4. **Follow up** - They're busy, persistence wins

**Eric's Practical Insight:**
> "Stop sending blind capability statements. Research the prime's current contracts, identify gaps, and show up with a solution. That's what gets callbacks."

---

#### 4D. EARLY ENGAGEMENT IS EVERYTHING

**The Industry Day Trap:**
> "If your first contact with a buying office is at Industry Day, you're already behind."

**What Winners Do:**
1. **Monitor forecast tools** - SAM.gov, GovWin, agency acquisition forecasts
2. **Request 1-on-1 meetings** - Before RFP drops
3. **Respond to Sources Sought** - Even if you won't bid
4. **Build relationships with SBLOs** - They're your advocates

**Eric's Practical Insight:**
> "The contractor who shapes the requirement wins the contract. Get in early. Ask questions. Offer solutions. By the time the RFP drops, you should already know what's in it."
