## PRESENTATION OUTLINE

### SECTION 1: WHERE WE ARE (5 minutes)
- Opening hook: "I talk to hundreds of small businesses every week..."
- $759B spent in FY2025 (largest ever)
- 26.5% small business share ($170B+)
- FAR being rewritten, NDAA FY2026 reforms

### SECTION 2: THE RULE CHANGES THAT MATTER (15 minutes)
- **FAR Overhaul:** Rule of Two is SAFE, 8(a) lock removed
- **NDAA FY2026:** CAS thresholds raised ($2.5M → $35M)
- **CMMC 2.0:** Now appearing in contracts, no self-attestation

### SECTION 3: WHERE THE A/E/C MONEY IS (15 minutes)
- **USACE:** $17B+ (Civil Works, Infrastructure Law, Harbors)
- **NAVFAC:** $8.5B+ (MILCON, SIOP, MACCs)
- **VA:** $2.7B PACT Act + $50B deferred maintenance
- **Air Force:** $33B backlog
- **Coast Guard:** $7B shore infrastructure

### SECTION 4: HOW TO WIN (10 minutes)
- **AI Advantage:** Proposal drafts in hours, capture research at scale
- **LinkedIn:** COs are watching, visibility = Source Sought inclusion
- **Teaming:** Navy SeaPort NxG is 85% small business
- **Early Engagement:** Shape requirements BEFORE RFP

### SECTION 5: THE OPPORTUNITY AHEAD (5 minutes)
- DoD budget up $113B
- Compliance thresholds raised
- Pain points = your opportunity
- Call to action: certifications, CMMC, vehicles, visibility

## KEY TALKING POINTS

### Eric's Signature Insights
1. "When I started, the complaint was 'government moves too slow.' Now it's 'I can't keep up with the changes.' That's actually a good problem."
2. "CAS threshold raised from $2.5M to $35M. The government is saying: we want you to grow. Take them up on it."
3. "I had a contractor pass on a $3M task order because they couldn't prove CMMC readiness. The CO moved on. Don't let this be you."
4. "USACE is desperate for contractors who understand digital delivery. BIM capability puts you ahead of 80% of competition."
5. "NAVFAC is one of the most small-business-friendly commands in DoD. If you're not on a MACC, you're missing $950M in task orders."
6. "AI doesn't replace relationships. It frees you up to build more of them."
7. "The contractor who shapes the requirement wins the contract. Get in early."
