MYTH BUSTER

Government Contracts Take Too Long

Hi {{first_name}},

"Government contracts take forever to award."

Yes and no. Let me explain.

The myth: All government contracts take 12-18 months from opportunity to award.

The reality: It depends on the acquisition method.

FAST (days to weeks):

  • Credit card purchases (under $10K)
  • BPA/IDIQ task orders
  • Simplified acquisitions (under $250K)
  • 8(a) sole source awards

MEDIUM (1-3 months):

  • Small business set-asides
  • Simplified acquisitions over $100K
  • GSA Schedule orders

SLOW (6-18 months):

  • Full and open competition over $250K
  • New IDIQ/GWAC on-ramps
  • Major system acquisitions

The strategy:

If you need revenue quickly, focus on:

  1. Subcontracting (primes pay faster than the government)
  2. Simplified acquisitions and micro-purchases
  3. Getting on existing contract vehicles (GSA, agency BPAs)
  4. 8(a) sole source if you have the certification

If you can wait for bigger wins, pursue the larger set-aside competitions while your quick wins provide cash flow.

The key insight:

Your BD pipeline should have a mix of short-term and long-term opportunities. Don't put all your eggs in one basket.
