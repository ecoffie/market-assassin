COMMON QUESTION

I Don't Have Past Performance

Hi {{first_name}},

The #1 objection I hear from new contractors:

"But I don't have any government past performance!"

I get it. It feels like a Catch-22. You need past performance to win contracts, but you need contracts to get past performance.

Here's the truth: you probably have more relevant experience than you think.

What counts as past performance:

  • ✓ Private sector work (especially with large enterprises)
  • ✓ State and local government contracts
  • ✓ Subcontracts under federal primes
  • ✓ Work for quasi-governmental organizations
  • ✓ Even relevant volunteer/pro-bono work

How to position non-federal experience:

In your capability statement and proposals, focus on:

  • The PROBLEM you solved (similar to federal agency problems)
  • The SCOPE (show you can handle the contract size)
  • The RESULTS (quantified outcomes)
  • The RELEVANCE (draw clear parallels to the federal requirement)

Quick wins to build past performance:

  1. Subcontract - Partner with primes who need your capabilities
  2. Micro-purchases - Contracts under $10K have minimal requirements
  3. 8(a) sole source - If you're 8(a), agencies can sole source up to $4.5M
  4. Simplified acquisitions - Contracts $10K-$250K have easier requirements

The path exists. You just have to start walking it.
