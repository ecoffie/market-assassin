The OSBP Connection Strategy

Hi {{first_name}},

There's someone at every federal agency whose literal job is to help small businesses like yours win contracts.

They're called the Office of Small and Disadvantaged Business Utilization (OSDBU) or Small Business Specialist.

And here's what most contractors don't realize:

These people have GOALS to meet.

Every agency has small business contracting goals mandated by Congress. If they don't hit those goals, they get called out publicly. So these specialists are actively looking for capable small businesses.

They can:

  • Tell you about upcoming opportunities before they're posted
  • Introduce you to contracting officers
  • Help you understand the agency's buying patterns
  • Give you feedback on your capability statement

How to find OSBP contacts (DIY method):

  1. Google "[Agency Name] OSDBU" or "[Agency Name] small business office"
  2. Visit the agency's official website and look for "Small Business" or "OSDBU" in the menu
  3. Check SBA.gov - they maintain a directory of agency OSDBU offices
  4. Look on LinkedIn for "[Agency Name] Small Business Specialist"

Once you find them, reach out with a simple email:

Subject: Introduction - [Your Company] - [Your NAICS Code]

Hi [Name],

I'm [Your Name] with [Company]. I did my market research and see that [Agency] buys the services we provide - specifically [what you do].

We've successfully delivered [brief credential] and are looking to support [Agency's] mission.

Would you have 15 minutes for a brief call to learn about your agency's upcoming needs?

By the way, I am a [set-aside type] business.

Best regards,
[Your Name]

Simple, professional, to the point.
