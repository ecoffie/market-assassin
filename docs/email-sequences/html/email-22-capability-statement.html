Your capability statement is your one-page business card for government contracting.

Most capability statements are terrible. They read like this:

"XYZ Corporation is a full-service provider of innovative solutions leveraging best-in-class methodologies to deliver synergistic outcomes..."

Nobody knows what that means. Nobody cares.

Here's what works:

Section 1: What You Do (2 sentences max)

"We help [type of agency] solve [specific problem] through [your approach]. Our clients save [specific benefit]."

Section 2: Core Capabilities (bullet points)

  • Capability 1 (with specific outcome)
  • Capability 2 (with specific outcome)
  • Capability 3 (with specific outcome)

Section 3: Past Performance (3 projects)

  • Client, Scope, Result
  • Client, Scope, Result
  • Client, Scope, Result

Section 4: Company Data

  • DUNS/UEI, CAGE, NAICS codes
  • Set-aside certifications
  • Contract vehicles

Section 5: Contact

Key contact with direct line

That's it. One page. Scannable. Clear.
