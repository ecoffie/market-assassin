The secret to winning government contracts:

It's not about having the lowest price.

It's not about having the most experience.

It's not even about who you know.

It's about understanding what problems the agency needs solved.

Every agency has "pain points" - challenges they're struggling with, mandates they need to meet, initiatives they're pushing forward.

When you understand these pain points, you can:

  • Position your solution as the answer to THEIR problems
  • Speak their language in proposals
  • Build relationships by offering genuine value
  • Stand out from competitors who just pitch features

Here's how to find pain points (DIY method):

  1. Read the agency's Strategic Plan (Google "[Agency Name] Strategic Plan")
  2. Check GAO reports at gao.gov for audits and recommendations
  3. Review Congressional testimony from agency leadership
  4. Look at OIG (Inspector General) reports for known issues
  5. Search for agency mentions in the NDAA (National Defense Authorization Act)

This research takes time - usually several hours per agency. But it's worth it. This is the intel that wins contracts.
