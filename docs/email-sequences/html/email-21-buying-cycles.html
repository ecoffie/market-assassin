Understanding Agency Buying Cycles

Hi {{first_name}},

Not all times are equal for business development.

Here's the federal buying cycle:

October-December (Q1)

  • New fiscal year starts
  • New budgets being allocated
  • Good time for introductions and relationship building
  • Limited new contract awards (budget still being distributed)

January-March (Q2)

  • Budgets are set
  • Requirements being defined
  • Solicitations starting to drop
  • PRIME TIME for BD meetings and positioning

April-June (Q3)

  • Major contracting activity
  • Most solicitations released
  • Agencies planning for year-end spending
  • Good time to be in active pursuit of opportunities

July-September (Q4)

  • Year-end spending surge
  • Quick-turn requirements
  • Agencies racing to obligate funds
  • Less time for new relationships, more transactional

What this means for you:

Your BD activities should align with these cycles:

  • Q1: Plant seeds, build relationships
  • Q2: Position for requirements
  • Q3: Actively pursue opportunities
  • Q4: Execute and capture quick wins

Right now, where are you in this cycle? What should you be doing?
