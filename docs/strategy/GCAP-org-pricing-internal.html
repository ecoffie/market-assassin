How we price the GCAP license — and why $36K

For GCAP (Government Contracting Assistance Program, a division of the NC SBTDC) — a capability-building program, not a DoD-metrics shop. The number is built only from our own published prices and GCAP's real footprint — not from any prior unaccepted quote or competitor number. Three independent methods converge on the same figure. Use this to quote consistently and to explain the number if asked.

01

What the license is

One flat annual org license — the complete Mindy platform for GCAP's office (pipeline, teaming, 317K contractor DB, all intel, client-workspace management, briefings, alerts, white-label, capability/funder reporting), minus Proposal Assist. The 12 certified procurement counselors get seats and manage their businesses' workspaces; businesses aren't sold individual seats. Proposal Assist is carved out because it burns real tokens — it's a metered add-on or counselors bring their own LLM. GCAP's value is capability-building (opportunity → assess ability → gap → readiness plan), not contract-chasing — the baseline features below reflect that.

02

The anchor

$36K/yr

GCAP — 8 counselors today, adding 4 (→ 12), ~1,000 businesses served

Confirmed footprint: 8 counselors on staff, 4 more being added (→ 12). Anchor the license to the 12 they're committed to, not today's 8 — you license the account they're becoming, so there's no true-up in 3 months when the 4 seats land. Flat annual, prepaid. This is a LIST anchor — multi-year and prepaid discounts (2yr ~8%, 3yr ~12%) are the concessions a rep trades to close.

Why this survives scrutiny: the convergence uses zero rejected/inferred anchors. It's the number our own economics produce — which is why it holds when the earlier quote-derived numbers don't.
Honest caveat: two inputs are judgment calls, not facts — the 1.5–2× management premium and the $2.50–3/workspace rate. They're what produce $36K. Nudge them up (2.5×, $4/wksp) and you land higher. $36K = "our real economics under sensible assumptions," a strong anchor to test — not a law.
On the 8→12 seat count: the seat-basis methods (1, 2) run on 12 — the team GCAP is committed to, not today's 8. Anchor to 12 and the $36K holds cleanly. If they push to land at 8 now, ~$24–28K is the floor with the 4 adds as built-in expansion — but that leaves money on the table they've already decided to spend and invites a true-up gov buyers dislike. Recommended: quote 12, frame it as "built for your full team, including the four you're adding."
Do NOT anchor on these (all discarded): the prior $50K/$68K quote — never accepted, proves nothing. The $35K/$50K ladder — derived from that rejected quote. "$150K State-Network" — inference from an unrelated association doc (1,000 clients ≠ 1,000 paid seats). 06

Recurring buyer concerns (every org raised)

•Capability, not wins — GCAP reports businesses served + milestones hit, NOT DoD contract metrics. Lead with progression, not the 35% mandate.
•Federal-first scope — say it upfront — GCAP serves local/state/federal; Mindy's data is strongest federal. Don't imply full state/local coverage.
•Report must map to GCAP's SBTDC/SBA fields — confirm exactly what they report before building the report (discovery Q1).
•Procurement even at $0 — the $0 60-day eval exists to skip the acquisition cycle
•Don't compete with Neoserra / Center IC — feed their CRM, don't replace it
•Budget is grant / fiscal-cycle dependent — annual prepaid, align to SBTDC's cycle; confirm who signs off
