## The Core Philosophy

> "To do great things, we must do fewer things. And unless we accept that, it gets very very hard to build a scalable business."

**The fundamental truth:** If you think being a million-dollar business is complex, imagine a $100 million business. The complexity is not linear—it is extremely exponential. Your job is to figure out the few things you can do and do them extremely well.

**Growth by subtraction, not multiplication:** The best businesses are simple: "This happens, this happens, and I make a lot of money. That's it."

## The Traffic-Systems-Skills Framework

In a service business, there are only **three things** that matter:

| Component | Job | Key Question |
|-----------|-----|--------------|
| **Traffic** | Fill the funnel with opportunities | Do we have an unlimited source of leads filling the funnel constantly? |
| **Systems** | Convert opportunities into appointments, cash, contracts | Do those leads automatically get converted? |
| **Skills** | Deliver, fulfill, and get paid | Is someone delivering in a highly skilled way? |

### How to Diagnose Your Business

1. **Overlay the framework** on your business
2. **Identify the constraint** - Most often it's the owner doing all the delivery (skills problem)
3. **Fix in order**: Systems FIRST, then Traffic
   - Why? Bad systems + lots of traffic = bad customer experience + wasted ad spend
   - Think: "Should we build the plumbing or send water through the pipes first?"

### The Assumption Phase

Before focusing on any area, **assume you've accomplished that goal**:
- "Let's assume I already have all the leads I want. Does everything work?"
- If no: identify what else needs to change BEFORE pursuing that goal
- This prevents getting stuck after achieving your objective

## The 1-1-1 Model

**The foundation for a $300,000 business:**

| One | Element | Example |
|-----|---------|---------|
| 1 | Traffic Source | Paid ads |
| 1 | Conversion Method | One-on-one call |
| 1 | Delivery Channel | Facebook group |

### Why 1-1-1 Works

- **Clarity**: When something breaks, you know exactly where
- **Diagnosability**: "My conversion isn't doing well" → Watch 10 calls, fix the script
- **Optimization**: Change three things in your script → funnel automatically works better

### Scaling Beyond 1-1-1

Once 1-1-1 is working:
1. Add a second traffic source
2. Add a third traffic source
3. When it breaks → probably need a different conversion source

**Traffic source categories:**
- Organic
- Paid
- Activity/Joint Venture (door knocking, flyers, partnerships)

## Building a Scalable Business Model

### The Don Shula Principle

> "Every single play that they mapped out ended up in a touchdown."

Most coaches plan: "Run this play, get 8 yards."
Don Shula: Every play had a **possibility of hitting a touchdown.**

**Apply to business:**
- Don't just plan for incremental progress
- Project out: "If I continue to do this thing, is it sustainable enough to grow?"
- If you're working 14 hours/day at $3M, you can't scale to $30M the same way

### The Projection Questions

Ask yourself:
- **"What would it take for this to be $10 million?"**
- **"What would it take for this to be $100 million?"**

When you put this frame on it, you realize:
- The current model doesn't work
- The complexity starts to fan out
- What's important vs. what's not becomes clear

## The Curse of Capability

> "Smart, capable entrepreneurs will put themselves in situations to do complex things because they're capable of doing those things."

**The Problem:** Capability creates complexity. The more you CAN do, the more you DO—and the harder it becomes to scale.

**The Solution:** Invest in "lazy" entrepreneurs:
- They find the easiest possible way to make money
- They avoid complexity by design
- They build businesses that run without them

### The Portfolio Company Approach

When acquisition.com invests in complex businesses:
- "Cut this, cut this, cut this, cut this, cut this, cut this"
- Result: 90% of time back, only 10% revenue drop

## Finding Your "One Day a Week"

### The Telus Story

**Context:** $300M real estate company with 30 agents

**The Questions Asked:**
1. "What is the ONE thing if I took away, you would not want to work here anymore?"
2. "What is the ONE thing you wish I added that would make you stay forever?"

**The Answer (unanimous):** "Being here saves me at least one day a week."

### The Transformation

**New value proposition:** "You should come to Telus because our job is to save you at least one day a week. What would you do with another 52 days a year?"

**Result:** $300M → $3.4 BILLION (10x in 5 years) with ONE value prop

### How It Simplified Everything

Every decision became simple:
- "Does this help our agents save more than one day a week?"
- YES → Do it
- NO → Don't do it

Example: "$10 million ad budget request?"
- Does this help agents save one day a week? No. → Rejected.

### The Mailchimp Example

**Mailchimp's one thing:** First 5,000 contacts FREE

While every other CRM charged $39-$100/month, Mailchimp said: "Why pay when you can start free?"

**Result:** Sold to Salesforce for billions—because of ONE differentiator.

## Building for Exit (Even If You Don't Plan to Sell)

> "The goal is not the exit. The goal is OPTIONS. When you don't know your options, you don't have any."

### The Annual "Soft Shop" Strategy

**Process:**
1. Every summer, take business to market as if actually selling
2. Identify 3-5 key potential buyers
3. Put together full sale package
4. Ask each: "How would you value us?"

### The Feedback Loop

**Conversation example:**
- Buyer A: "You're worth $50M"
- You: "Why not $75M?"
- Buyer A: "To be $75M, you need these 5 things"
- **Action:** Those 5 things become next year's business plan

**Next year:**
- "You said if I did these 5 things, it would be $75M"
- "You did! Great. Why not $150M?"
- "To get $150M, you need these 6 things"

### What Buyers See

After 2-3 years of this process, potential buyers see:
1. **Malleability**: Business can grow with new strategies
2. **Confidence in operator**: Team can implement and execute
3. **Reduced risk**: Track record of adaptation

### Bonus Insight

Potential buyers also tell you **what's NOT valuable**:
- "We didn't give Orange County any value—it's losing money for these three reasons"
- **Action:** Deprioritize that part of business

## The Three Types of Growth Options

| Option | Description | Trade-off |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Self-funded** | Fund growth yourself because you believe $1M → $10M | You own everything |
| **Outside equity** | Bank, private equity, or investor provides capital and resources | Co-ownership but more resources |
| **Employee ownership (ESOP)** | Sell to employees (they may only contribute 10%, bank covers rest) | Creates 50 vested shareholders who won't leave |

### The Wealth Management Firm Example

- Sold 50% of business to employees
- Employees contributed 10%, bank covered other 40%
- Owner received $50M
- **Later:** Private equity paid premium because "50 shareholders, all vested, zero defection risk"
- **Final result:** $50M check → $250M check (all employees got a piece)

**Best for:** Services businesses where people stay long-term (consulting, accounting, etc.)

## Phantom Equity Strategy

**Problem:** Giving real equity is complex:
- Change LLC/corporation docs
- Shareholder rights
- Tax structure changes
- Liability if employee leaves or does something crazy

**Solution:** Phantom Stock Program

### How It Works

| Real Stock | Phantom Stock |
|------------|---------------|
| 100 shares owned by you | Create separate "Phantom Stock Program" |
| Employee gets actual ownership | Employee gets document that mirrors real stock |
| Tax consequences, liability, rights | No liability, no tax consequences, no rights |
| Stays if employee leaves | Goes away if employee leaves |

**The pitch:** "If we ever sell this business, you get 10%. But you have no liability, no tax consequences. If you leave, this goes away."

**Benefits:**
- Employee feels like owner
- Builds loyalty
- Attracts A-players
- Creates future exit optionality

## The Memo Culture

> "No memo, no decision. No memo, no meeting."

### Why Memos Matter

- **Organized thinking** creates clarity of results
- Most people have never been taught to organize their thoughts
- Fear has no place on paper—you see it and it's not scary anymore

### The Three-Part Memo Framework

**Part 1: Story So Far**
- Write TO your coach (someone with no context)
- List 7 things that have happened
- Create shared understanding

**Part 2: The Issue**
- Based on story, here's the problem we're solving
- Here's my recommendation

**Part 3: Open Questions**
- I don't know the answers to these 5 questions
- Can we meet?

### Meeting Protocol

1. Deliver memo BEFORE the meeting
2. Require people to read with shared understanding
3. Meeting = only discuss questions in the memo
4. Result: 10x more productive meetings

### Institutional Knowledge Benefit

- New employee? "Read our last 20 memos"
- Similar project? "Duplicate that memo, write a new one"
- It's like an SOP where decisions are already made

### Using AI to Create Memos

1. Voice chat your disorganized thoughts
2. Say: "Put this in the memo framework"
3. AI reorganizes into thoughtful framework
4. Share with coach, advisers, team

## Hiring A-Players

### The Only Two Reasons to Hire

1. **Solve the pain of today** - "I'm drowning, I need help yesterday"
2. **Enable the growth of tomorrow** - "Wouldn't it be amazing if we had this person?"

### Why A-Players Are Hard to Find

**Root cause:** You don't articulate exactly what you need.

### The AI Job Description Method

**For pain of today:**
1. Write down ALL the pain:
   - No time for this
   - Poor quality here
   - This sucks
   - Clients having bad experience
   - Stressed, can't sleep
2. Upload to AI: "Turn all of this pain into a job description"

**For growth of tomorrow:**
1. Write down all aspirations:
   - Wouldn't it be amazing if we could do A, B, C, 1, 2, 3
2. Upload to AI: "Turn all my aspirations into a job description"

### Why This Works

- A-players actually READ job descriptions
- They need to see YOUR puzzle piece to know if their piece fits
- An A-player for you ≠ an A-player for someone else
- It's about FIT

## The Decision-Making Framework

> "Most people don't have a framework for making decisions. Therefore, they make dumb decisions."

### The Four-Step Framework

| Step | Action | Question |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1 | **Understand context** | What is the shared information? |
| 2 | **Isolate the issue** | What problem are we actually solving? |
| 3 | **Accept the risk** | If we make this decision, what actually breaks? |
| 4 | **Map the decision** | What are the next steps? |

### In Practice

1. "Can you tell me about that?" (Understand facts)
2. "So the problem we're trying to solve is THIS" (Isolate issue)
3. "If we make this decision, we probably can't do these projects" (Accept risk)
4. "Given all that, what is the next step?" (Map decision)

## The 10-10 Forever Rule

> "The biggest misconception about building wealth is us thinking that it is a WHAT and HOW problem and not a WHO problem."

### The Framework

**Question:** Who are the 10 people you would invest in for the next 10 years who would pay you forever?

Instead of:
- Chasing the next hot stock
- The next hot startup

Do this:
- "I'm going to put [NAME] in my 10-10 forever"
- "Whatever she says, I'm going to do"
- "Whatever I can do to support her, I'm going to do"

**Truth:** Wealth creation is a WHO strategy, not a HOW strategy.

## Freeze Lifestyle Principle

> "The number one thing an entrepreneur can do to set themselves up for scale in the early part of their lives is to FREEZE LIFESTYLE."

### The Trap

| Revenue | Take-home | Need to hire | Problem |
|---------|-----------|--------------|---------|
| $500K | $150K | $200K person | Can't afford them—would go to $0 |
| $1M | $300K | $250K person | Now used to $300K lifestyle—still can't hire |

**Result:** "I AM the service. I AM the delivery. If I pull myself out, I don't make the lifestyle I'm used to."

### The Solution

**Shaan's approach:** Same monthly budget for 14 years, despite net worth growing 50x.

**Why:**
- Everything above baseline = risk-adjusted capital
- Unlimited optionality
- Can always make the moves that matter

**The problem with lifestyle creep:** Very easy to turn UP lifestyle, very hard to turn DOWN.

## Key Principles from the Anatomy of a Dream

### 1. Skill Stacking

Combine multiple unrelated skills to form unique advantage:
- Tennis → Discipline, performing under pressure
- Computer Science → Reverse engineering problems
- Goldman Sachs → Deal structure knowledge
- Real Estate → Networking, marketing, CEO skills

**Key insight:** The most powerful skills are often forced on you or picked up to survive. Necessity builds a better stack than intention.

### 2. Proximity

Your environment shapes your outcomes.

**Action:** Place yourself in environments that:
- Elevate your thinking
- Accelerate your pace
- Expand what you believe is possible

### 3. Asking for Help

> "To solve any problem, ask: What could I do? What could I read? Who could I ask?" — Jim Rohn

**The $10,000 Coach Email:**
> "I would like to offer you $10,000 as a symbol of my seriousness if you can be my coach for the year. I don't expect any meetings, scheduled phone calls, or to bother you in any way. I would just hope that you would prioritize responding to my emails."

**Result:** "Write it up." She became his first coach.

**Key:** Make it LOW STAKES for them. Don't say "pick your brain" or "take your time"—that feels heavy.

### 4. Recruiting Superstars

**For coaches/mentors:** What do they want?
- Some want money
- Some want ownership
- Some just want to be on a winning team

**For employees:** Ask them directly:
- "What is ONE thing if I took away, you wouldn't want to work here?"
- Use THAT as your recruitment pitch

### 5. Attention Management

> "What are the 2-3 areas of my business that have the most payoff when I do them?"

**The 80/20 rule:**
- Spend 80% of time on top 3 things
- Spend the MOST time on #1
- Renew this assessment every quarter

### 6. Simplicity as Strategy

**Two advantages:**
1. Easier to create scalable systems
2. Easier to market and sell

**Stats:** 76% of people are more likely to recommend brands with simple experiences. Simplicity-leading brands outperform stock market by 200%.

**Examples:**
- Good American started with just jeans
- Skims started with just shapewear
- Instagram was only square photos
- Drybar was only blowouts

### 7. Finding Bottlenecks

**Key question:** If your business doubled tomorrow, where would it break?

**Second-order thinking:** Assume everything works. What breaks next?

**The Mr. Beast approach:** Even with his face on everything, he pops in for a few hours across multiple episodes. He found and systematized around the bottleneck.

### 8. Save for a Rainy Day

> "Do not save what is left after spending. Spend what is left after saving." — Warren Buffett

**Buffett still lives in the same house he bought in 1958 for $31,500.**

**Key question:** If your revenue stopped tomorrow, how many months could you survive? (Target: 6 months)

### 9. The Exit Mindset

Build as if someone could buy tomorrow—not because you plan to sell, but because that standard creates:
- Something that doesn't need you
- Clear systems
- Real value

**Apple example:** Steve Jobs built something so systematized it didn't just survive without him—it's still thriving.

### 10. Undeniable Proof

Do something so well that people can't ignore you. The data speaks for itself.

**Close the imagination gap:** Don't ask them to imagine what you could do—SHOW them.

### 11. Find Your Allies

> "The person you marry is the most important business decision you'll ever make."

**Cut out:**
- People who make you keep your dream to yourself
- People who make you feel guilty for working on weekends
- People who make your dream feel too big

**Keep:** People who remind you what you're capable of—especially when you forget.

### 12. Persistence

> "Not giving up is the most heroic thing you can do." — Aquaman poster

**The truth:** Success isn't just about winning. It's about who you are when you lose.

**Chiron's losses:**
- Dumpster diving first week in America
- Mugged on first night
- Diluted on first exit
- Lost $1M in bad business deal
- Years of uncertainty building Telus

**The pattern:** He kept moving forward.
