A few questions so we build the right thing for GCAP

Before we scope or price anything, we want to confirm what we already understand about GCAP and fill the gaps — how you actually work and report, not what we assume. Your answers shape what we build. Short answers are fine; anything you'd rather talk through, we'll cover on a call.

We want Mindy to fit that, and to produce the reporting your funder expects. These questions make sure we align to your reality before we commit to a plan.

The reporting you need to produce

This is the one we most want to get right — if Mindy produces the numbers you already have to report, it becomes the system that renews your funding, not just another tool. Tell us exactly what that report looks like.

1.What do you report to your funder (SBTDC / SBA) as success — and who is the audience for it?
e.g. businesses served, counseling hours, milestones reached, capital accessed, jobs created, contracts won. And who reads it — your center director, SBTDC state HQ, SBA?
2.In what format, system, and cadence does that reporting live today?
e.g. EDMIS, Neoserra exports, a spreadsheet you hand-build, a quarterly PDF — and monthly / quarterly / annually. If we can generate it in that shape, we save you the assembly.
3.Which capability milestones count most — the events that, when a business hits them, prove your program worked?
e.g. SAM registration, a certification (WOSB / HUBZone / 8(a)), a completed capability statement, a first bid submitted, a first award. These are what a progression dashboard would track per business.
4.If you could open one dashboard and see your whole caseload's progress at a glance, what would you want on it?
The 3–4 numbers or views that would actually change how you run the program — this is what we'd build first.
