# Mindy Stage Strategy — Training → Tools, Brand → Category

**Premise (Eric):** "Elon made money making others money by building things of value.
Do the same for Mindy." We scaled the *training* Eric built; now scale the *tool*. We
already have brand + reach. Goal: get Eric on **major GovCon stages discussing tools,
not training** — positioned as the GovCon-tech founder, not the GovCon trainer.

**Created:** 2026-06-19.

---

## The reframe: what would Elon do?

Elon doesn't chase conference invites. Three moves, mapped to Mindy:

1. **Build the stage, don't beg for it.** His biggest stages are his own product unveils.
   We already own a stage with reach (GovCon Giants podcast/YouTube + 8,803 bootcamp
   leads + ~9,900 users). Turn it into the must-watch Mindy demo, and the circuit comes
   to us. External stages = amplification, not the strategy.
2. **Demo > talk, always.** Never a "10 tips" panel. Stand next to the thing doing the
   impossible. Our version: a **live Mindy demo that makes a contractor money on stage**
   — real expiring vehicle → response generated in 30s → real $ surfaced.
3. **Make the customer the hero.** "Make others money" literally: put a contractor who
   WON real money with Mindy on stage. Their win, their number, our real data. Beats any
   feature keynote 10:1.

**The training→tools shift IS this:** stop teaching the audience to fish; show the
machine catching fish, live.

## "Mindy Day" — the owned unveil (build this FIRST)

Rename the bootcamp-style event to an Elon-pattern **product unveil**. Working name:
**Mindy Day** (like Battery Day / AI Day — the product is the headline, recurring +
ownable: Mindy Day 2026, 2027…). Tagline: *"Mindy Day — watch it find you money."*

**Format (unveil, NOT bootcamp):**
1. Open cold on a LIVE demo — real expiring $X vehicle, response generated in 30s. No
   "here's the agenda."
2. A real customer who WON tells their win (dollar figure + Mindy screenshot).
3. Unveil ONE new capability each Mindy Day (the reason to show up — e.g. hidden-match,
   semantic search).
4. The audacious, data-backed claim: "$X in opportunities surfaced for our users this
   quarter."
5. Close = the offer (try Mindy / start a pilot), NOT "sign up for training."

**Why first:** the recording becomes the proof reel that earns the *keynote* invites
below — instead of the *training-track* invites the trainer brand attracts today.
