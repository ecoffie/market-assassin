# GovCon Market Research Framework

*Based on GAO-15-8 and FAR Part 10 guidance*

## Overview

Market research is the foundation of successful federal capture. The government conducts market research before every procurement - **companies that actively participate in this process gain competitive advantage.**

---

## The 4 Basic Market Research Elements (GAO)

Every federal market research effort should address:

| Element | What It Covers | Why It Matters for Contractors |
|---------|----------------|-------------------------------|
| **1. Methods Used** | RFIs, sources sought, database searches, industry days | Know which channels the agency uses - that's where you need visibility |
| **2. Timeframes** | When each method was employed | Understand the procurement timeline to position early |
| **3. Vendor Capability Analysis** | Assessment of market capabilities | Demonstrate your capabilities match the requirement |
| **4. Conclusion** | Decision based on analysis | The government's conclusion shapes acquisition strategy |

### Contractor Action by Phase

**Presolicitation (6-18 months out)**
- Monitor agency forecasts on acquisitiongateway.gov
- Respond to Sources Sought / RFI notices immediately
- Attend industry days and capability briefings
- Build relationships with program office staff

**Preaward (RFP imminent or released)**
- Download and analyze draft RFP documents
- Submit clarification questions
- Finalize teaming arrangements
- Lock in pricing and past performance

**Postaward (on existing vehicles)**
- Stay visible for task order competitions
- Demonstrate price reasonableness
- Maintain relationships with contracting officers

## Market Research Techniques

### What the Government Uses

| Type | Techniques | Your Response |
|------|------------|---------------|
| **Internal** | Prior acquisition history, government databases, catalogs | Ensure your past performance is documented |
| **External - Passive** | RFIs on SAM.gov, Sources Sought notices | Respond to EVERY relevant notice |
| **External - Active** | Industry days, vendor meetings, site visits | Attend and present capabilities |

### GAO Finding: Industry Outreach = Better Competition

> *"For contracts of $10 million or more that involved communicating with industry through RFIs, industry days, or meetings, 9 of 12 received multiple offers."* - GAO-15-8

**Translation:** When you respond to RFIs and attend industry days, you're more likely to be in a competitive position.

## Market Research Best Practices for Contractors

### 1. Early Engagement (Critical)

**DO:**
- Respond to Sources Sought within 48 hours
- Attend every industry day for target agencies
- Request capability briefings with program managers
- Monitor forecasts 12-18 months ahead

**DON'T:**
- Wait for the RFP to drop
- Assume they know your capabilities
- Skip Sources Sought because "it's just research"

### 2. Capability Statements

Your capability statement should directly address:
- [ ] Relevant NAICS codes (exact match)
- [ ] Past performance on similar contracts
- [ ] Key personnel qualifications
- [ ] Small business certifications
- [ ] Clearances held

### 3. RFI Response Template

```
SECTION 1: Company Overview
- Name, UEI, CAGE, Size Status
- Primary NAICS codes
- Certifications (8(a), SDVOSB, WOSB, HUBZone)

SECTION 2: Relevant Experience
- 3 similar contracts (agency, scope, value, dates)
- Key differentiators

SECTION 3: Technical Approach
- How you would address the requirement
- Unique capabilities or solutions

SECTION 4: Teaming
- Prime/sub partnerships in place
- Gap-filling strategy
```

### 4. Industry Day Preparation

Before attending:
- [ ] Research the program and incumbent
- [ ] Prepare 3 specific questions about requirements
- [ ] Bring updated capability statement
- [ ] Identify teaming targets who will attend
- [ ] Plan follow-up within 48 hours

## Applying This to Capture Strategy

### In Daily Briefs (Active Opportunities)
- Flag opportunities with Sources Sought or RFI stage
- Highlight response deadlines
- Note if industry day scheduled

### In Weekly Deep Dives (Recompete Intel)
- Identify contracts entering market research phase
- Track agency forecast postings
- Flag Sources Sought notices for expiring contracts

### In Pursuit Briefs (Capture Strategy)
- Recommend specific market research actions:
  - "Respond to Sources Sought by [date]"
  - "Request capability briefing with PM"
  - "Attend [Agency] industry day on [date]"
- Score opportunities higher if market research engagement possible
