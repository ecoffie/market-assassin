### Email 4: Day 4 - Education (How to Read Opportunities)

**Subject:** 90% of contractors read opportunities wrong. Here's the fix.

---

Hey {{firstName}},

Quick story: A contractor once told me they skipped an opportunity because it said "experience required." They had no federal experience.

They didn't read the fine print. "Experience" meant commercial experience. They were qualified. They missed a $400K contract.

**Here's how I read opportunities (and how you should too):**

**1. Check the set-aside FIRST**
If it's set aside for 8(a) and you're not 8(a), move on. Don't waste time.

**2. Read the Description, not just the Title**
Titles are generic. "IT Support Services" could mean anything. The description tells you if it's help desk (boring) or cybersecurity (your specialty).

**3. Look for the Incumbent**
The opportunity won't say "Acme Corp has this now." But I can tell you. Knowing the incumbent tells you if this is a recompete (harder) or new work (easier).

**4. Check the Response Date**
15 days = they probably have someone in mind. 45 days = real competition welcome.

**5. Find the Contact**
The Contracting Officer's name is gold. Google them. Find them on LinkedIn. Understand who you're selling to.

**I do steps 1-4 automatically in your briefings.** Step 5 is on you - for now. 😉

→ [See today's opportunities]({{dashboardLink}})

— Mindy
