Email Scripts for SBLO Outreach

5 ready-to-use email templates for connecting with Small Business Liaison Officers

Initial Introduction

1
When to Send: First contact with a new SBLO. Send Tuesday-Thursday between 9-11 AM ET for best open rates.
Subject Line
Introduction: [YOUR COMPANY] - [CERTIFICATION] Small Business in [YOUR INDUSTRY]
Email Body
Dear [SBLO NAME], My name is [YOUR NAME], and I am the [YOUR TITLE] of [YOUR COMPANY], a [CERTIFICATION - e.g., 8(a) certified, WOSB] small business based in [CITY, STATE]. We specialize in [YOUR PRIMARY SERVICES/PRODUCTS] and have been supporting federal agencies for [X] years. Our capabilities include: - [Core Capability 1] - [Core Capability 2] - [Core Capability 3] I would appreciate the opportunity to introduce our company and learn about [AGENCY NAME]'s upcoming small business contracting opportunities. Would you be available for a brief 15-minute call or virtual meeting in the coming weeks? I have attached our capability statement for your review. Please feel free to share it with any contracting officers who may benefit from our services. Thank you for your time, and I look forward to hearing from you. Best regards, [YOUR NAME] [YOUR TITLE] [YOUR COMPANY] [PHONE] | [EMAIL] [WEBSITE]
Pro Tip: Always attach your capability statement. SBLOs often forward these to contracting officers who have active requirements matching your capabilities.

7-Day Follow-Up

2
When to Send: 7 days after initial email if no response. Keep it short and add value.
Subject Line
Following Up: [YOUR COMPANY] - Small Business Introduction
Email Body
Dear [SBLO NAME], I wanted to follow up on my email from last week introducing [YOUR COMPANY]. I understand you are busy, so I will keep this brief. We recently [completed a relevant project / earned a new certification / won a contract with a similar agency], which demonstrates our ability to support [AGENCY NAME]'s mission. I am flexible on timing and would welcome even a brief 10-minute conversation to introduce our capabilities. I am also happy to provide any additional information about our company via email if that is more convenient. Thank you again for your time. Best regards, [YOUR NAME] [YOUR COMPANY] [PHONE]
Pro Tip: Don't just repeat the first email. Add something new - a recent win, certification, or relevant news about their agency to show you've done your research.

Industry Day / Event Request

3
When to Send: When you learn an agency is hosting an industry day, or proactively ask about upcoming events. Check agency forecast pages quarterly.
Subject Line
Upcoming Small Business Events at [AGENCY NAME]?
Email Body
Dear [SBLO NAME], I hope this message finds you well. I am reaching out to inquire about any upcoming industry days, small business matchmaking events, or vendor outreach sessions that [AGENCY NAME] may be planning. [YOUR COMPANY] is a [CERTIFICATION] small business specializing in [YOUR SERVICES]. We are eager to participate in any opportunities to connect with [AGENCY NAME]'s procurement team and learn about upcoming requirements. Specifically, I am interested in: - Any scheduled industry days or pre-solicitation conferences - Small business matchmaking events - Subcontracting partnership opportunities with current prime contractors Could you please share information about any upcoming events, or direct me to the appropriate point of contact? Thank you for supporting small businesses in federal contracting. Best regards, [YOUR NAME] [YOUR TITLE] [YOUR COMPANY] [PHONE] | [EMAIL]
Pro Tip: Many agencies publish forecast lists on their OSDBU websites. Reference a specific upcoming opportunity when possible to show you've done your homework.

Subcontracting Directory Request

4
When to Send: When targeting subcontracting opportunities. SBLOs often maintain lists of prime contractors required to meet subcontracting goals.
Subject Line
Request: Subcontracting Opportunities at [AGENCY NAME]
Email Body
Dear [SBLO NAME], I am writing to inquire about subcontracting opportunities with prime contractors currently serving [AGENCY NAME]. [YOUR COMPANY] provides [YOUR SERVICES] and we are actively seeking teaming and subcontracting partnerships. Our NAICS codes include [PRIMARY NAICS CODES]. Could you please: 1. Share a list of prime contractors with active subcontracting plans at [AGENCY NAME] 2. Recommend any prime contractors who may benefit from our capabilities 3. Advise on the best way to get listed in your agency's small business vendor database We hold the following certifications: [LIST CERTIFICATIONS], which may help prime contractors meet their small business subcontracting goals. I have attached our capability statement for your reference. Thank you for your time and support. Best regards, [YOUR NAME] [YOUR TITLE] [YOUR COMPANY] [PHONE] | [EMAIL]
Pro Tip: SBLOs are required to help small businesses connect with prime contractors. They often know which primes are looking for specific capabilities. Be specific about your NAICS codes and certifications.

Relationship Maintenance

5
When to Send: Every 60-90 days to keep your company top of mind. Best sent after a company milestone or when you have new information to share.
Subject Line
Update from [YOUR COMPANY] - [BRIEF UPDATE]
Email Body
Dear [SBLO NAME], I wanted to share a quick update from [YOUR COMPANY]. Since we last connected: - [Recent accomplishment - e.g., We successfully completed a $X contract with [AGENCY]] - [New capability - e.g., We expanded our team with [X] new certified professionals] - [Certification update - e.g., We recently obtained our [NEW CERTIFICATION]] We remain interested in supporting [AGENCY NAME]'s mission and would welcome any opportunities to discuss upcoming requirements or connect with contracting officers in the [SPECIFIC AREA] space. I have attached our updated capability statement for your files. Thank you for your continued support of small businesses. Warm regards, [YOUR NAME] [YOUR TITLE] [YOUR COMPANY] [PHONE] | [EMAIL]
Pro Tip: Consistency is key. SBLOs hear from hundreds of companies. Regular, value-add updates keep you top of mind when relevant opportunities arise. Always include your updated capability statement.
