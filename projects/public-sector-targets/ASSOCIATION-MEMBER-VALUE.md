## The commercial model

This is **not** a referral program where members sign up at retail and the association gets a kickback.

The association **acquires usage rights** to Mindy (annual org license) and **extends access to members** as a differentiated benefit — the same pattern as [`docs/proposals/USHCC-Atlanta-Mindy-Proposal.html`](../../docs/proposals/USHCC-Atlanta-Mindy-Proposal.html) and Coach Mode ([`docs/PRD-coach-mode-apex.md`](../../docs/PRD-coach-mode-apex.md)).

```
Association buys license → acquires N member seat rights + coach dashboard + board report
→ members activate entitlement (no retail checkout) → association proves ROI quarterly
```

## The member problem (hidden loss)

Members are not losing because they lack skill. They are losing because:

1. **Visibility** — They cannot see where federal dollars flow in *their* trade (NAICS, agency, set-aside lane).
2. **Qualification** — They chase full-and-open competitions against primes instead of set-asides and SAT/micro lanes under $350K.
3. **Process** — They do not understand SAM registration, forecasts, incumbents, and recompete timing.

**Reframe (JTED / case studies):** *"You don't have a qualification problem. You have a visibility problem."*

**Set-aside blindness example** ([`presentations/case-study-framework.md`](../../presentations/case-study-framework.md)):

> Client was bidding full-and-open against large primes and losing on price every time. They had SDVOSB certification sitting unused. There were 23 SDVOSB set-asides in their NAICS posted in the last 90 days — they'd responded to zero. That's $47M in addressable pipeline they didn't know existed.
