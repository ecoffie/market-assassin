## 1. The one-paragraph version

Mindy is our AI GovCon intelligence platform (daily opportunity briefings, 317K-contractor
search, market research, proposal assist). We already sell **"Coach Mode"** — an org license
that lets an organization run Mindy for a whole roster of small businesses. This initiative
extends that to the organizations that **fund and run federal small-business programs**:
APEX Accelerators, SBDCs, economic development corporations (EDCs), state minority-business
divisions, and NMSDC councils. We sell them a platform that helps them **prove outcomes to
their funders** and **match their clients to contracts/partners** — and we fund our own R&D
with non-dilutive grants (SBIR/EDA/SBA) on the side.

**Two revenue lines:**
1. **Org licenses (primary)** — $18K chamber → $50K–$200K EDC/state contracts.
2. **Non-dilutive grants (fuel)** — SBIR/EDA/SBA fund the build, not the bottom line.

---

## 2. Why this works — the wedge

We are **not** selling "free alerts." Every competitor does that. Our wedge is four things
these organizations can't get elsewhere and have to report on anyway:

1. **317,106 contractor profiles** — find primes, incumbents, and teaming partners by NAICS,
   agency, set-aside, and state. (Counselors do this by hand in SAM/LinkedIn today.)
2. **DoD Critical Technology Area (CTA) filters** — APEX centers must report **35% CTA
   alignment** to keep their DoD funding. We tag opportunities to the 14 CTAs automatically.
3. **Teaming / Find Partners** — match a small minority firm to a larger prime in the same lane.
4. **Win attribution by roster (UEI)** — prove *their* clients won contracts ("your program
   placed 12 firms into $4.2M of DoD work"), not generic industry stats. *(Building — live by NAPEX.)*

**The pitch line (use it verbatim):**

> *"We don't just help your clients find opportunities — we help you prove placement into the
> defense supply chain and match them to the right prime partners in Critical Technology Areas."*

**Mantra:** we take **reporting + matching** dollars, not **counseling** dollars. We *partner*
with APEX/SBDC (they do 1:1 coaching; we're the data + outcomes layer), we don't replace them.

### Two strategic calls the research surfaced (important context)

- **⛔ MBDA centers are NOT a reliable channel right now.** A 2025 Executive Order gutted MBDA;
  agreements were terminated, a court ordered reinstatement, the administration appealed (Jan
  2026), and **all agreements expire Aug 31, 2026.** Don't pitch anything badged "MBDA center" —
  go to the **surviving operator under its own name** (often an NMSDC council) + the **state division**.
- **💡 State-certification programs are a LEAD channel, not a backfill.** Every GovCon SaaS fights
  at the crowded federal door. State SB/MBE/HUB programs are **un-served by opportunity-intel
  tooling**, have **captive rosters of certified firms + recurring vendor forums** (built-in
  distribution), and feed a natural **state→federal upsell**. This is where we make a splash away
  from the crowd. *(Honest note: these certify for STATE contracting — our angle is "graduate your
  certified firms to federal.")*
