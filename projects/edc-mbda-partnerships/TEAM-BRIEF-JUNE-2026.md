## 1. The one-paragraph version

We sell **Mindy** (our AI GovCon intelligence platform) to two kinds of buyers: **(A) organizations
that serve contractors** — APEX Accelerators, SBDCs, economic development orgs (EDCs), minority-
business councils — who license "Coach Mode" to help their clients; and **(B) government offices
directly** — contracting and small-business offices that need Mindy's data to do their own jobs.
Track A is the partnership/funnel play. **Track B is new, hot, and already has three warm
government contacts with working prototypes.** We fund product R&D with non-dilutive grants on the
side, but the revenue is software sales.

**Two revenue lines:** org/agency licenses (primary) + non-dilutive grants (fuel, not income).

---

## 2. Why we win — the wedge

Not "free alerts." Four things competitors don't offer, that our buyers must produce anyway:
1. **317K contractor profiles** — find/rank firms that have actually won the work (USASpending).
2. **PSC + NAICS relevance scoring** — match by *what was bought*, not just industry code, so we
   never miss a capable firm (accuracy: score, don't filter).
3. **Set-aside / small-business intelligence** — who's small, who's won set-aside work.
4. **DoD Critical Tech Area tagging + recompete/expiry intelligence.**

**Mantra:** we take **reporting + matching + sourcing** dollars, not counseling dollars. We partner
with APEX/SBDC, we don't replace them.

## 4. How we sell it — the strategy (the Anduril model)

We studied how Anduril started. The lesson (verified): they didn't avoid SBIR — they refused to be
**grant-dependent**. Build a real product → sell it fixed-price as a commercial item to an end-user
with a budget → use SBIR Phase III / OTA to *scale* a thing that already works.

That maps onto us. So:

**TRACK 1 — SELL DIRECT (revenue now).** The 3 warm contacts are BUYERS, not grant sponsors. They
buy Mindy as a commercial-item subscription:
- U.S. Army Contracting Command – Orlando → micro-purchase (≤$15K) pilot → simplified acquisition (≤$350K). The KO can buy it.
- DISA → DITCO or the Tradewind marketplace.
- Navy OSBP → micro-purchase + NavalX Tech Bridge.
- **Pilot shape:** small ($15K), 60–90 days, one team → land → expand to adjacent offices.

**TRACK 2 — ONE SBIR for the strategic key (not income).** Landing any one SBIR unlocks **uncapped,
no-recompete Phase III sole-source across every agency** (the exact lever Anduril used). Vehicle:
AFWERX Open Topic — needs an Air Force end-user (our 3 are Army/Navy, so we target an AF office like
AFICC/AFLCMC). This is a parallel, longer play — not the near-term revenue.

**The universal close (every gov conversation):** don't ask for a contract — ask for their real
codes/requirement + a **no-cost pilot on their data.**
