## The profile (be precise — wrong target wastes the runway)
**NOT** a DCAA/FAR compliance CPA (those help *contractors* bill the government). **YES** a
**federal financial-statement / agency-side audit-readiness** expert who knows:

These people live at: the big federal-audit-readiness contractors, FASAB/Treasury alumni, ex-DoD
financial-management (FM) officers, and AGA (Assoc. of Government Accountants) circles.

## The 4 channels, priority order

### 1. ⭐ Ex-DoD/DLA financial-management (FM) officer — BEST fit
Someone who did DLA or DoD audit-readiness from the *inside* (J8/comptroller/FM, recently retired or
in industry). They know SFFAS 47 in practice AND the DLA environment + the TPOCs' world.
- **Where:** LinkedIn — search "DLA" OR "DFAS" + "audit readiness" / "financial improvement" / "FIAR"
  (Financial Improvement and Audit Readiness program); "comptroller" + "Defense Logistics Agency".
  AGA (agacgfm.org) chapters, esp. DC/NCR. Retired-military FM networks.
- **Ask:** advisor/consultant on the proposal + Phase I (named key personnel). Doesn't need to be a
  full subcontractor — a credentialed named advisor with real DLA-FM background is gold to evaluators.

### 2. Small federal-audit-readiness firm (subcontractor / teaming partner)
A small business whose bread-and-butter IS federal financial-statement audit readiness (FIAR support,
SFFAS implementation). They co-sign as a sub; you prime (keeps the SBC-prime SBIR structure clean).
- **Known names in the agency-audit-readiness space (verify each — do NOT assume):** Kearney & Company,
  Cotton & Company (Sikich), Castle/Castro & Co., Williams Adley, CliftonLarsonAllen (CLA) federal,
  Guidehouse/Kearney-tier. Many are mid/large; find the *small-business* ones or a small boutique.
- **Where:** SAM.gov entity search — NAICS **541211 (CPA offices)** / **541219** + set-aside +
  keyword "audit readiness" / "financial statement audit"; USASpending — who DLA/DFAS already pays for
  audit-readiness support (search recipients on DLA/DFAS audit contracts). *(Mindy can run this.)*

### 3. AGA / CGFM credential network
The **Association of Government Accountants** is the professional home of federal accountants; **CGFM**
(Certified Government Financial Manager) is the credential. An advisor with CGFM + federal-reporting
experience is exactly the signal. AGA National Capital Region chapter = densest pool near DLA (HQ Ft.
Belvoir, VA / NCR).

### 4. University / STTR route (if no individual SME lands)
If you can't secure an individual/firm SME in time, an **STTR** (vs SBIR) lets a **research institution**
(university accounting dept / a federal-accounting research center) be the partner — but STTR has its
own rules + the topic must offer an STTR variant. Slower; fallback only.
