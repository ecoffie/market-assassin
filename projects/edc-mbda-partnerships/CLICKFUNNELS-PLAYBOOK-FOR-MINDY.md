## The core idea: the VALUE LADDER (not a pricing page)
Brunson's #1 framework. A sequence of ascending-value offers; each step delivers, then naturally
leads to the next. Mindy's value ladder:
| Rung | ClickFunnels | Mindy |
|---|---|---|
| **Lead magnet (free)** | free book/report/challenge | **Free daily alerts** + a lead-magnet (e.g. "Your NAICS Opportunity Report", "The 2026 Recompete List") |
| **Frontend (low $)** | $7 book, free+shipping | a cheap unlock or a one-time report / mini-course |
| **Core (continuity)** | $97/mo ClickFunnels | **Mindy Pro $149/mo** (the recurring core) |
| **Backend (high $)** | $25k coaching / events | **Team/Enterprise, lifetime, done-for-you, the bootcamp** |
The free tier isn't charity — it's rung 1 of the ladder. Every free user is climbing.

## The 4 acquisition engines Brunson used (all = Mindy's lane, NONE = enterprise sales)
1. **Webinar funnel** — his single biggest. A free live "reveal" → soft pitch → trial. **Mindy = the
   June 27 bootcamp/launch IS this.** Systematize it: recurring free live workshops ("Find your first
   federal contract in 60 min with Mindy") → free signup → Pro. Run monthly, evergreen the recording.
2. **Book/content funnel** — give away the knowledge, upsell the tool. GovCon Giants ALREADY does this
   (content, YouTube, email). **Point the existing content machine at Mindy as the CTA.** A free
   "GovCon Secrets"-style lead magnet/book = top of funnel.
3. **Affiliate army** — ClickFunnels hit $1M on affiliates. **Mindy: turn the GovCon Giants audience +
   power users into affiliates/advocates** (you already have advocate accounts). Recurring commission
   on Pro referrals = a sales force you don't pay salaries for.
4. **Challenges + community** — "5-Day Lead Challenge" mechanic. **Mindy: a "5-Day Find Your First
   Contract Challenge"** (gamified onboarding) → daily email + a task in the app → habit + Pro upgrade.
   Community (FB group / Skool) = retention + word-of-mouth + content engine.

## The messaging frameworks (steal these directly)
- **The Attractive Character** — people follow a PERSON, not a SaaS. GovCon Giants brand = the
  attractive character (the "Nike of GovCon" moat). Lead with story, not features. *(Tension w/
  `exit_strategy_brand_separation`: GovCon Giants is the MARKETING character; Mindy is the clean
  product. Brand acquires; product is the asset.)*
- **Soap Opera Sequence** — a 5-email welcome drip that tells a STORY (hook → backstory → epiphany →
  hidden benefits → urgency), not a feature tour. Mindy's free-signup welcome series = this. (We have
  a free→paid drip; rewrite it Soap-Opera style — memory `marketing_email_voice` already says
  value-first, this is the structure.)
- **Seinfeld emails** — daily, entertaining, story-led emails that always tie back to a soft CTA. The
  GovCon Giants email voice, pointed at Mindy.
- **Hook → Story → Offer** — every ad, page, email. Mindy's FB/IG ads: a hook ("$82B unspent"),
  a story (a contractor who found a contract), an offer (free alerts).

## Gamification (Eric's lane — "where we live")
- **Onboarding as a game** — progress bar, "set up your profile → get your first match → unlock X."
  ClickFunnels onboards with quick wins. Mindy: streaks, "X opportunities found for you", badges.
- **The Challenge mechanic** — time-boxed, daily tasks, public accountability → completion → upgrade.
- **Leaderboards / social proof** — "1,200 contractors got alerts today", live counters (the bootcamp
  scarcity counter is already this).
