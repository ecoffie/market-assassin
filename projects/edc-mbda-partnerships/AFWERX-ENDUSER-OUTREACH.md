## Read this first — what we're actually asking for (verified)

The research corrected a common misconception. Get this right or the outreach reads wrong:

1. **The signed "Customer Memorandum" is required at PHASE II, not Phase I.** At Phase I, *finding*
   the end-user **is the work** — AFWERX even assigns "SAGE fellows" + a curriculum to help. So
   **we are NOT asking anyone to sign anything now.** We're asking: *"is this a real problem for
   your unit, and would you be open to exploring fit?"* The signature comes later, only if we
   advance and only on the solicitation-specific form.
2. **Who can be the end-user/customer:** someone who can **speak for their organization on
   operational needs, requirements, or funding** — a program office or operational unit. **No rank
   requirement.** Active-duty OR civilian both qualify.
3. **Who is a CONNECTOR, not a signer:** OSBP / small-business offices and APEX Accelerators
   generally **cannot** be the signer (they're advisory) — but they are exactly who you ask to
   *introduce* you to the right operational end-user. Two different asks → two different emails below.
4. **It's non-binding, costs them nothing, no funding commitment** — and a government employee
   endorsing an SBIR is **explicitly authorized by federal ethics law** (5 CFR 2635.702(c)
   Exception 1, which cites the SBIR statute 15 U.S.C. §638). Say this — it removes their #1 hesitation.
5. **Low-pressure fallback:** an interested-but-not-primary person can give a **Letter of Support**
   (not graded, not binding) instead of being a signer.

**The mission-need framing (lead with this, not the product):** give a DAF office **visibility into
small-business participation across the 14 Critical Technology Areas**, and help **strengthen the
defense industrial base** by getting more qualified small/minority firms into the supply chain.
That's a *government end-user* story — the vendor-tool framing is not.

## Etiquette guardrails

- **Lead with the mission need, not the product.** First question is always "is this a real problem
  for you?"
- **Never ask for a signature in a first contact.** Phase I = exploration.
- **Be explicit it's non-binding / no cost / ethics-authorized** — that's the unlock.
- **Don't conflate roles:** Customer/End-User just express interest; the TPOC takes on
  performance-monitoring after award. Don't over-ask.
- Use the **solicitation-specific** memo version only — never collect signatures on an old form.
