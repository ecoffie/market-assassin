## 🎬 THE PITCH

### STEP 0 — Frame (30 sec, before sharing)
> "When we talked, you said the hard part is getting scaleable access to small businesses you can
> actually contract with. So I'll show you exactly that — every small business that has *won* the
> kind of work you buy, ranked by how well they match, exportable to a list. Let me share my screen."

### STEP 1 — Search the work, not just the code (2 min)
**Do:** Type PSC **J998** (or whatever she buys) + NAICS **336611** → "Find businesses."
**Say:**
> "I'm searching by PSC — the actual product or service, ship repair — not just the industry code.
> That matters: a firm that's done the *work* is a real match even if they're registered under a
> slightly different NAICS. So we don't miss anyone."

**When results land (~4,594):**
> "Thousands of capable firms — not a 10-row sample. Every one has won federal work, ranked by
> relevance: the green ones won this exact service, then related work, then the industry."

### STEP 2 — "Capable AND small" (2 min) ← her core need
**Do:** Set the **≤ $25M** ceiling, check **"set-aside winners only."**
**Say:**
> "Now I bias it toward genuinely small firms — under a federal-dollar ceiling — and to the ones
> that have actually *won small-business set-aside work*. That's your sourcing pool: small,
> certified-active, and proven. Each row shows why it matched and its track record."

**Point at a row:**
> "This firm — won the exact work, set-aside winner, [X] contracts. That's a defensible source for
> your market research, not a guess."

### STEP 3 — The export (1 min)
**Do:** Click **"Export to CSV."**
> "And it's a list you can drop straight into your market research file or hand to a CO. The sourcing
> that used to take days of SAM and Google is one search."

### STEP 4 — The bigger picture (1 min) — optional
> "This same engine feeds the contracting officer's Market Research Report — the supplier and
> set-aside sections write themselves from this data. So whether it's you sourcing or a CO
> documenting, it's the same source of truth."

### STEP 5 — Close (1 min)
> "Two questions so I tailor it to PSNS&IMF:
> 1. What are the top few PSCs / NAICS you source against most? I'll pre-load them.
> 2. Would it help to also see *where* these firms are (region), and to filter to a size standard?
>
> Give me your real sourcing codes and I'll set this up on your data for a pilot — no cost to look."

**The ask:** her real PSC/NAICS sourcing codes + permission to set up a pilot view.
