## 🎬 THE PITCH

### STEP 0 — Frame (30 sec)
> "You sent me the Army Market Research Report template — the May 2026 version. I built Mindy to fill
> out the data-heavy sections of *that exact form* from real federal award data, so you get a first
> draft instead of starting from a blank 16-page document. It won't write your judgment calls — and
> I'll show you exactly where it stops. Let me share."

### STEP 1 — "Your form, one input" (1.5 min)
**Do:** Show the panel; type the requirement title + PSC + NAICS.
> "I give it what I'm buying — the PSC and NAICS — and the requirement title. That's it."
**Click "Generate MRR draft."**

### STEP 2 — The market picture, instantly (2 min)
**Do:** Point at the stat cards + the §12 recommendation.
> "In a few seconds: the supplier pool, how many are small businesses, how many have won set-aside
> work, and the prior contracts. And here's the §12 recommendation — 'Small business set-aside' —
> with the rationale: it found [N] capable small businesses, which meets the FAR Part 19 rule of two.
> That's your set-aside determination, grounded in real data."

### STEP 3 — The auto-filled tables (2.5 min) ← the payoff
**Do:** Scroll the §9 Procurement History preview.
> "§9 Procurement History — the prior contracts: who won, contract type, dollars, period. This is
> the table you normally pull by hand from FPDS. It's already built."
**Then mention §11:**
> "And §11 Potential Suppliers is filled with capable firms, ranked by who's actually done this work
> — same engine the small-business office uses for sourcing."

### STEP 4 — The actual Word document (2 min) ← the close-maker
**Do:** Click **"Download MRR (.docx)"**, open it in Word on screen.
> "And it exports as *your* template — the real MRR layout, with §5, §9, §11, §12, §15 filled in and
> sourced to USASpending. You open it in Word and finish it."

### STEP 5 — The honesty (1 min) ← what earns a CO's trust
**Do:** Point at the amber "you complete these sections" box.
> "Here's what Mindy does NOT do — and I want to be straight about this: your Independent Government
> Estimate, the commerciality determination, the requirement description, the signatures. Those are
> your judgment and your authority. Mindy does the research; you make the determinations. It brackets
> every one of those so nothing's faked in your contract file."

### STEP 6 — Close (1 min)
> "So this turns days of market research into a draft you finish in an hour. Two things to make it
> real for your shop:
> 1. Give me a live requirement — a PSC/NAICS you're working now — and I'll generate its MRR.
> 2. Is the May 2026 template the current one ACC-Orlando uses, or do you have local addenda? I'll match it.
>
> Then we run it on a real action as a pilot — no cost."

**The ask:** a real PSC/NAICS to run live + confirmation of the current template version.

## 🛡️ ANTICIPATED QUESTIONS

| He asks… | You say… |
|---|---|
| "Where's the data from?" | "USASpending federal award data — every figure is real obligations, dated. The supplier and history tables cite the source." |
| "Does it write the whole determination?" | "No — and that's deliberate. It fills the research/data sections; you write the IGE, commerciality determination, and sign. It never fabricates a determination." |
| "Is this authorized / can it go in my contract file?" | "It produces a draft you review, complete, and certify — same as any tool-assisted research. What goes in the file is your finished, signed document. Standing it up for ACC-Orlando use, with any IT review, is what a pilot scopes." |
| "It used the wrong NAICS / set-aside." | "You control the inputs — change the PSC/NAICS and regenerate. The recommendation follows the real data; if you disagree, your judgment overrides, and the doc is yours to edit." |
| "Does it handle the IGE?" | "No — the IGE is your cost estimate; it's bracketed for you to insert. It's not something a market tool should invent." |
| "What about the RFA / non-commercial determination?" | "Bracketed for you — that's a legal/policy call. Mindy gives you the market research that *supports* it." |

## 🚫 DON'T
- Don't claim it writes the IGE, commerciality determination, or any signature — those are bracketed.
- Don't claim an ATO/authorization it doesn't have — frame production as the pilot's job.
- Don't demo a blank result — pre-run a populated PSC/NAICS.
- Don't pitch the platform — this is *his MRR*, the one form he sent.

> the Word doc to finish."
