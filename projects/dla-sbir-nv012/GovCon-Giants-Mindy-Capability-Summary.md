## The opportunity

**DLA SBIR topic DLA26BZ03-NV012** — *AI-Powered Tool for Automated Evaluation of Vendor Economic
Dependency.* Phase I **$100K / 12 mo** → Phase II **$1M / 24 mo** → Phase III DoD-wide transition.
**Closes 22 July 2026.** The customer (DLA) is already attached.

DLA wants an AI tool that automatically assesses how economically dependent each vendor is on the
government — identifying **related parties and concentration risk per SFFAS 47** to support audit
readiness and supply-chain resilience. Today this is manual and can't scale across thousands of vendors
and millions of transactions.

---

## What we bring — built and proven

- **The data engine.** Mindy already computes **federal obligated dollars per vendor by UEI** across the
  entire USASpending universe (317K contractors) — the dependency *numerator* is a production
  capability, not a research risk. *(It returns McKesson's $84B and Pfizer's $28B in federal obligations
  instantly.)*
- **The public-financial join.** Built to ingest SEC EDGAR 10-K revenue + SAM.gov — the exact sources
  the topic names. Dependency % = federal $ ÷ total revenue.
- **Provenance by default.** Every figure carries its source and as-of date — the *auditable, traceable*
  results the topic and SFFAS 47 require.
- **The SBIR vehicle and delivery.** GovCon Edu primes, writes the proposal, and owns registration /
  ITAR / CMMC. The AI engineering is entirely ours — no IT or development partner needed.

### The AI capability behind it (production, not a prototype)

- **Grounding & citations** — every answer cites its source record and as-of date; answers that can't be
  grounded **abstain rather than fabricate.**
- **Hallucination controls** — retrieval-only context, citation enforcement, confidence gating, and an
  LLM-as-judge fact-check on high-stakes output.
- **Governed agentic automation** — bounded/scoped permissions, reversible operations, and full audit
  traceability on every autonomous action.
- **Security & sustainment** — least-privilege access, encryption in transit and at rest, full audit
  logging, and a provider-agnostic model fallback (no single-vendor lock-in).
