# Launch Memo Skill

**Status:** Draft  
**Owner:** GovCon Giants / Launch Ops  
**Purpose:** Convert scattered launch thinking into a clear memo the team can execute without needing repeated founder explanation.

## When To Use This

Use this skill for:

- Bootcamp launch planning
- MI Free or MI Pro launches
- White-glove offer launches
- Email campaign launches
- Product rollout announcements
- Team alignment before a major push
- Any situation where the team needs context, decisions, owners, and next actions

## Launch Memo Format

### 1. Story So Far

Summarize the facts that got us here.

Include:

- What changed in the business
- What customers are telling us
- What the data says
- What has already been shipped
- What problem is now urgent

### 2. Strategic Thesis

State the belief driving the launch.

Example:

> GovCon Giants is moving from training-led monetization to Market Intelligence software plus high-touch services. The launch should show customers how MI helps them find, evaluate, and pursue federal contracts, not teach another generic training lesson.

### 3. Customer Segment

Clarify who this launch is for.

- Audience only
- MI Free
- MI Pro
- Bundle customers
- Internal users
- 10-10 Forever candidates
- White-glove candidates

### 4. Core Outcome

What should the customer be able to do after this launch?

Examples:

- Complete their MI profile
- Find qualified contracts
- Save or track opportunities
- Understand a recompete
- Identify teaming partners
- Upgrade to MI Pro
- Apply for white-glove support

### 5. Offer / Ask

What are we asking the customer to do?

- Create account
- Complete profile
- Attend bootcamp
- Upgrade to MI Pro
- Book a call
- Join white-glove
- Refer a partner

### 6. Proof

What evidence supports this launch?

- User behavior
- Purchases
- Engagement data
- Customer quotes
- Case studies
- Contract wins
- Speaker/advisor credibility

### 7. Success Metrics

Define metrics before launch.

- Emails sent
- Opens/clicks
- Accounts created
- Profiles completed
- MI sessions
- Time in MI
- Opportunities saved
- Pipeline items created
- Calls booked
- Upgrades
- White-glove applications

### 8. Team Roles

| Owner | Responsibility | Due |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Founder | Strategic message and priority calls | TBD |
| Marketing | Email/social/landing page | TBD |
| Customer Success | Follow-up and profile completion | TBD |
| Engineering | Product readiness and instrumentation | TBD |
| Sales | Qualified customer outreach | TBD |

### 9. Risks

List the main risks and mitigations.

Examples:

- Email deliverability issue
- Confusing offer
- Broken login/setup flow
- Dashboard numbers misread
- Customers do not complete profiles
- Team sells training instead of outcomes

### 10. Decisions Needed

Make open decisions visible.

- What is the exact CTA?
- Who receives the first email?
- What access tier do they get?
- What is the deadline?
- Who owns follow-up?

### 11. Next Actions

Every memo ends with concrete action.

| Action | Owner | Due | Status |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Draft launch email | Marketing | TBD | Open |
| Pull qualified customer list | Sales | TBD | Open |
| Verify MI login flow | Engineering | TBD | Open |

## Rules

1. Do not write launch memos as inspiration only.
2. Every launch memo must include customer segment, offer, metrics, risks, and next actions.
3. Avoid vague CTAs like "check it out."
4. Do not mix MI Free, MI Pro, and white-glove without explaining the path.
5. If the launch does not help users find or win contracts, clarify why it matters.

## Definition Of Done

A launch memo is ready when the team can answer:

- What are we launching?
- Who is it for?
- Why does it matter now?
- What should the customer do?
- What does success look like?
- Who owns each part?
- What could break?
- What happens next?
