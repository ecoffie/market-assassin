# GovCon Offer Reframing Skill

**Purpose:** Keep GovCon Giants offers framed around customer outcomes instead of legacy product names, training language, or disconnected tools.

Use this skill when writing website copy, emails, launch memos, dashboards, onboarding, pricing pages, or internal team updates.

## Core Positioning

GovCon Giants is no longer primarily a training company.

The new frame:

> GovCon Giants helps small businesses find, understand, pursue, and win federal contracts they would not have found or acted on alone.

Market Intelligence is the core software product. Training, community, services, and white-glove support exist to help customers get outcomes from the platform.

## Offer Ladder

| Tier | Who It Serves | Promise | Primary CTA |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| MI Free | Audience and early users | Find relevant opportunities and start receiving alerts | Complete profile |
| MI Pro | Serious operators | Daily intelligence, research, forecasts, recompetes, contractors, pipeline, and team workflows | Subscribe / use weekly |
| MI Internal | Team/admin users | Operate, test, support, and improve MI without billing confusion | Manage customers |
| Bundle / Ultimate | Existing paid customers | Consolidated access to MI and owned tools | Set up account |
| White-glove | Committed clients | Done-with-you or done-for-you pursuit support | Strategy call |

## Naming Rules

- Use **Market Intelligence** as the umbrella product.
- Use **MI Free** for free opportunity alerts and basic access.
- Use **MI Pro** for paid intelligence and workflow access.
- Use **MI Internal** only inside admin/team docs, not customer-facing copy.
- Treat old tool names as feature/history labels, not the main offer.
- Avoid leading with "Market Assassin" in new customer-facing surfaces unless referencing legacy access.

## Outcome Language

Prefer:

- Find federal contracts
- Understand which opportunities are worth pursuing
- Track pursuit stages
- Find teaming partners
- Prepare proposals
- Save research time
- Get daily intelligence
- Win contracts you would not have found on your own

Avoid:

- Learn everything about government contracting
- More training modules
- Another course
- 11 separate tools
- Generic AI platform

## Segment Messaging

### MI Free

Message:

> Start receiving federal opportunity alerts that match your business profile.

Primary action:

> Complete your profile so the system can send better matches.

Do not overpromise full intelligence, forecasts, exports, team workflows, or deep research.

### MI Pro

Message:

> Run your GovCon business development workflow from one place: briefings, alerts, forecasts, recompetes, contractor research, pipeline, and teaming.

Primary action:

> Use MI weekly to identify, evaluate, and act on qualified opportunities.

### Bundle / Ultimate

Message:

> Your previous purchase gives you access to the unified MI platform. Set up your account so your tools, alerts, briefings, and profile work together.

Primary action:

> Create password, complete profile, confirm access.

### White-Glove

Message:

> If you are ready to pursue serious contracts, GovCon Giants can help with capture, proposal, and execution support.

Primary action:

> Book a strategy conversation.

## Copy Checklist

Before publishing copy, check:

- Does it say what the customer can do?
- Does it clarify who the offer is for?
- Does it separate free, pro, bundle, internal, and white-glove?
- Does it avoid legacy tool confusion?
- Does it avoid making training the headline?
- Does it map to a next action?

## Dashboard / Admin Labels

Use labels that clarify action:

- `MI Free active alerts`
- `MI Pro active subscribers`
- `Profiles completed`
- `Profiles need setup`
- `Pro users using MI this week`
- `Free users ready to upgrade`
- `White-glove candidates`

Avoid labels that mix inventory, entitlement, send history, and engagement without context.

## Email Template Rules

Every onboarding or reset email should say:

- Product name: Market Intelligence
- Why they are receiving the email
- What action to take
- What access level they have, when relevant
- Where to go next

Do not let Supabase default templates say unrelated legacy products such as "GovCon Content Generator."

## Done Criteria

An offer is ready when a customer can answer:

1. What is this?
2. Is it for me?
3. What result does it help me get?
4. What do I do next?
5. What do I get if I upgrade?
