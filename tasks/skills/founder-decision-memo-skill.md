# Founder Decision Memo Skill

**Status:** Draft  
**Owner:** Eric / Leadership  
**Purpose:** Convert high-stakes founder thinking into a clear decision memo with context, tradeoffs, risks, and next actions.

## When To Use This

Use this skill when a decision affects strategy, pricing, access, launch timing, product direction, or team ownership.

Trigger examples:
- "Before we design it do we need to plan out what I'm thinking?"
- "How should we handle current users?"
- "Should Free users get this?"
- "What should be public versus gated?"
- "Are we eliminating .org?"

## Memo Structure

### 1. Story So Far

What happened that led to this decision?

Include:
- Relevant dates
- Customer/team signals
- Product changes
- Revenue/access implications
- What has already been decided

### 2. The Decision

State the decision in one sentence.

Example:
> All MI users will use email + password; 2FA is optional, not required.

### 3. Options Considered

| Option | Upside | Downside | When It Works |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |

### 4. Recommended Path

Explain why this option best fits the current strategy.

### 5. Risks

- Customer confusion
- Team confusion
- Engineering complexity
- Security risk
- SEO risk
- Revenue/access risk

### 6. Decision Levers

What can we change if the data says the decision is wrong?

### 7. Owner Map

| Owner | Responsibility | Due |
| --- | --- | --- |

### 8. Communication Plan

Who needs to know?

- Internal team
- Coaches
- Sales
- Social/content
- Existing users
- New users
- Paid users

### 9. Definition Of Done

What proves the decision was implemented?

## Decision Quality Checklist

- Does this reduce confusion?
- Does this help users find or win contracts?
- Does this improve the MI Free to MI Pro path?
- Does this protect customer data?
- Does this reduce founder bottlenecks?
- Is the team able to act from this memo?

## Output Format

Produce:

- One-page decision memo
- Summary Slack version
- Owner/action table
- Open questions
- Follow-up measurement plan

## Definition Of Done

A founder decision is clear when the team can execute without re-asking:

- What did we decide?
- Why did we decide it?
- Who owns it?
- What changed for users?
- What changes if the data disagrees?
