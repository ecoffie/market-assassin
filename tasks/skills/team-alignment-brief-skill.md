# Team Alignment Brief Skill

**Purpose:** Turn founder thinking, product decisions, dashboard findings, and launch changes into concise internal briefs the team can act on without needing Eric to repeat the context live.

Use this skill for Slack posts, team memos, launch updates, weekly briefs, and handoffs.

## When To Use

Use this skill when:

- A strategy changes.
- A launch plan changes.
- A dashboard reveals something confusing or important.
- A product decision affects multiple people.
- Annelle/Sikander need outreach context.
- Engineering ships or changes customer-facing behavior.
- The team needs to know what to stop doing.

## Brief Structure

### 1. Headline

One sentence that says what changed.

Example:

> GovCon Giants is moving from training-first to Market Intelligence as the core product.

### 2. Why This Matters

Explain the business or customer reason.

Keep it direct:
- What problem does this solve?
- What customer behavior caused this decision?
- What risk does this reduce?
- What opportunity does this create?

### 3. What We Are Doing Now

List the current operating decision.

Examples:
- Customers first, advisory second.
- MI Free users get profile setup and basic alerts.
- MI Pro users should not receive redundant free alerts.
- White-glove candidates come from behavior, not theory.

### 4. Who This Affects

Identify segments:

- MI Free
- MI Pro
- Bundle / Ultimate
- Internal users
- White-glove candidates
- Team members
- Admin / support
- Engineering

### 5. What Each Team Member Does

Use owner/action language.

| Owner | Action | Due |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Annelle | Contact A1/A2 customer list | This week |
| Sikander | Log outcomes and product feedback | This week |
| Engineering | Protect route / fix dashboard / update copy | This sprint |
| Eric | Call top 10-10 candidates | This week |

### 6. Metrics We Are Watching

Choose only the metrics that matter for the decision.

Examples:
- New users
- Profile completion
- MI active users
- Time in MI
- Briefing opens/clicks
- Helpful rate
- Pipeline adds
- Teaming adds
- Upgrade candidates
- White-glove candidates

### 7. Data Quality Notes

If numbers may be confusing, say why.

Examples:
- This is a 7-day count, not all-time.
- This is entitlement inventory, not usage.
- This excludes internal users.
- This includes free users.
- This dashboard is reading from delivery logs, not subscriptions.

### 8. Decisions / Open Questions

Separate decided items from open items.

**Decided:**
- MI Pro should not receive redundant MI Free emails.

**Open:**
- Should 2FA remain optional for all users or required for admins?

### 9. Next Update

Say when the next check-in happens and what should be ready by then.

## Slack Version

When posting in Slack, shorten to:

1. **Headline**
2. **What changed**
3. **What we need each person to do**
4. **What metric we are watching**
5. **When we check again**

## Memo Version

When writing a full memo, include:

- Story so far
- Current issue
- Recommendation
- Risks
- Team actions
- Open questions
- Decision owner

## Done Criteria

A team brief is ready when:

- The team knows what changed.
- The team knows why it matters.
- Every action has an owner.
- Metrics map to decisions.
- Data caveats are explicit.
- The brief reduces follow-up clarification instead of creating more.
