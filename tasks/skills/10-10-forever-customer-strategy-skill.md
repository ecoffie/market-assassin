# 10-10 Forever Customer Strategy Skill

**Purpose:** Identify the customers, users, operators, and proof relationships GovCon Giants should invest in deeply for the next decade.

This skill turns messy customer evidence into a short ranked list of people worth personal attention, product input, white-glove offers, case studies, referrals, or long-term partnership.

## When To Use

Use this skill when deciding:

- Who Eric should call personally.
- Who belongs in the next 10-10 Forever list.
- Which customers should shape MI product direction.
- Which users deserve white-glove, team, or enterprise outreach.
- Which stories should become proof for May 30, MI launch, SEO pages, or sales.

## Core Belief

Wealth creation is a WHO strategy, not a HOW strategy.

For MI, that means:

- Do not chase every free user equally.
- Watch behavior.
- Invest in customers who buy, show up, configure, reply, use the platform, ask serious questions, and move toward outcomes.
- Treat inactive free users as audience until their behavior says otherwise.

## Inputs

Use as many of these as available:

- Stripe purchases and subscriptions
- MI Free and MI Pro entitlements
- Profile completion and custom NAICS data
- Briefing opens, clicks, helpful/not helpful feedback
- App activity and time in MI
- Pipeline, saved opportunities, teaming, proposal, and win signals
- Webinar, bootcamp, livestream, and replay attendance
- Customer support replies and call notes
- Existing 10-10 Forever notes
- White-glove, bundle, and high-ticket purchase history

## Qualification Signals

Score people on behavior, not potential alone.

| Signal | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Bought high-ticket or bundle | Willing to invest |
| Subscribed to MI / Briefings | Values recurring intelligence |
| Configured profile / custom NAICS | Took activation action |
| Opened or clicked briefings | Engages with product output |
| Saved/tracked opportunities | Moving toward a business result |
| Asked serious questions | Has intent and context |
| Attended live events | Shows up |
| Replied to outreach | Reachable and collaborative |
| Has contract history or clear niche | Can become a proof story |
| Brings team or partner network | Can expand account value |

## Priority Bands

### A1: Personal Founder Call

Use when the person has both commercial value and strategic proof value.

Examples:
- High-ticket buyer
- Ultimate Bundle buyer
- MI Pro subscriber with profile configured
- Existing customer with contract history
- Active user who could become a case study

### A2: Annelle / Sikander Interview

Use when the person is qualified enough for discovery but does not require Eric first.

Examples:
- Recent purchaser
- Active alert/briefing user
- Lower-ticket buyer with strong engagement
- Customer who clicked or replied

### B: Nurture and Observe

Use when someone has some value but not enough current action.

Examples:
- Free user with partial setup
- Tool buyer without recent activity
- Subscriber who has not configured profile

### Hold: Audience Only

Use when behavior does not justify human time yet.

Examples:
- No profile
- No opens/clicks
- No purchase
- No reply
- No recent activity

## Output Format

Create a ranked table:

| Rank | Person / Email | Segment | Evidence | Recommended Action | Owner | Timing |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Then add:

### Top 5 Personal Calls

Who should Eric call and why?

### Top 20 Customer Interviews

Who should Annelle/Sikander contact first?

### Proof Candidates

Who could credibly show that MI helps small businesses find or win federal contracts?

### Product Feedback Candidates

Who can tell us which MI features matter most?

### White-Glove Candidates

Who is likely ready for done-with-you or done-for-you execution?

## Call Questions

Ask the two Wealth GENIUS questions first:

1. What is the one thing about GovCon Giants that, if we took it away, would make this no longer valuable to you?
2. What is the one thing you wish we added that would make you stay forever?

Then ask MI outcome questions:

1. What kind of contracts are you trying to find?
2. What makes you decide an opportunity is worth pursuing?
3. What part of the capture or proposal process slows you down most?
4. What would make MI feel like it saved you one day a week?
5. What would need to happen for you to say MI helped you win?

## Decision Rules

- If a user is paid but inactive, send setup help before pitching.
- If a user is active and paid, ask for a call.
- If a user is active and unpaid, show the upgrade path.
- If a user has a team, discuss team workflow and Deal Flow Board.
- If a user has contract history, ask for proof/story permission.
- If a user asks for execution help, evaluate white-glove fit.

## Done Criteria

This skill is complete when the team has:

- A ranked customer list.
- Clear owners and next actions.
- A smaller founder-call list.
- A larger customer-interview list.
- Notes that can feed product, launch, sales, and proof assets.
